# Teach your agent to push your thinking further before locking in an answer

**What this unlocks**
Your agent already generates ideas. This skill teaches it to run a proper brainstorm: expanding possibilities before evaluating any of them, contributing angles you aren't already considering, naming ideas before killing them, and ending with a synthesis question that surfaces what actually changed. You stop getting a list of obvious options and start getting thinking that genuinely opens up the problem.

**Try it now**
- "I need to brainstorm how to solve this: [your challenge]. Push me past the obvious options before we narrow down."
- "Give me feedback on this idea. Be specific about what works and name the one thing to change."
- "Stress-test this plan: what assumption does it depend on, and what happens if that assumption is wrong?"

**What to ask after**
- "I have three directions. Help me choose based on what I'm optimizing for."
- "Red team this: argue against it so I can find the weakest point before someone else does."
- "I keep coming back to the same two options. What am I not seeing?"
- "Restate what I'm actually deciding, then give me options with trade-offs. No paragraph narratives."
- "What do I now believe that I didn't believe at the start of this conversation?"

**Tune it to your world**
Tell your agent the kinds of decisions you make most often: product direction, hiring, budget, strategy. Tell it who else is usually in the room. A solo brainstorm runs differently than prep for a room full of skeptics.
