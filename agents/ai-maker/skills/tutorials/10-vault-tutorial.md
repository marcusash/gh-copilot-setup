# Teach your agent to remember what matters

**What this unlocks**
Your agent knows what you tell it in a session. This skill teaches it to save things permanently: decisions you've made, guides for processes you run repeatedly, proposals waiting on review, references you look up constantly. The vault is your working memory. After this skill, "save this for later" actually means something.

**Try it now**
- "Save this decision to the vault: [state the decision]."
- "I keep forgetting how to do this. Save a how-to for me."
- "What's in my vault? Summarize it."

**What to ask after**
- "File this proposal somewhere I'll find it before the meeting next week."
- "Do I have anything saved about this topic?"
- "I made this decision. Record it so we don't revisit it."
- "Save these steps as a how-to. I'll run this process again next quarter."
- "The vault is getting full. Help me decide what to remove."

**Tune it to your world**
Tell your agent which categories matter most for your work. A manager running weekly reviews will vault how-tos and recurring references. A project lead will vault proposals and decisions. The vault works for any rhythm; you just tell it which items are worth keeping.
