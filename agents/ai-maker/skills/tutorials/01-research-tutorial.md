# Teach your agent to investigate, not guess

**What this unlocks**
Before this skill, your agent answers questions with what it already knows. After this skill, it investigates first: checking evidence, separating what it found from what it inferred, and telling you exactly how confident it is. You stop getting conclusions dressed as answers. You start getting findings you can act on.

**Try it now**
- "Why do I keep running out of time before my Friday deadline? Investigate and give me a finding."
- "I think my meeting prep is the bottleneck. Verify that before giving me a recommendation."
- "Look at the last three projects I've described to you. What pattern do you see? How confident are you?"

**What to ask after**
- "Investigate whether this decision is based on assumptions that could be wrong."
- "What would have to be true for this plan to fail? Check it before we commit."
- "I've heard this claim. Research it and give me a confidence level."
- "Before we decide: what evidence would change our answer?"
- "Summarize what the data shows versus what you're inferring from it."

**Tune it to your world**
Tell your agent which domains you make important decisions in (hiring, budget, product bets, customer calls) and it will apply this methodology there first. The more context you give about what you need to decide, the more targeted the investigation.
