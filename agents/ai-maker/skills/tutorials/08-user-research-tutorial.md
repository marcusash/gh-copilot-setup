# Teach your agent to see your work through the eyes of the person who will actually use it

**What this unlocks**
Your agent can review things from inside your perspective. This skill teaches it to step out of your perspective entirely and walk through your work as someone who wasn't there when you built it. It names where a first-time user gets stuck, classifies whether each problem is a full stop or just friction, and separates what needs fixing from what should stay exactly as it is.

**Try it now**
- "Walk through this as someone who has never seen it before. Where do they get stuck?"
- "Here's the audience: [describe them]. What would they assume about this that isn't actually true?"
- "I want to ship this. Do a quick usability check and tell me anything that's a full stop before I send it."

**What to ask after**
- "Build me a specific persona for the person I'm designing this for. Not a generic one."
- "Walk through this step-by-step as the persona. What do they see, understand, and do at each step?"
- "Which of these issues would actually stop someone versus just slow them down?"
- "What is this experience assuming the user already knows? Are those assumptions correct?"
- "What should I definitely not change? What's already working?"

**Tune it to your world**
Tell your agent who your users are: their role, their comfort level with technology, what they're afraid of, and what they do when something goes wrong. The more specific the person, the more specific and useful the findings.
