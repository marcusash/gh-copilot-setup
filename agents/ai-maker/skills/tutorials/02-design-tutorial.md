# Teach your agent to design things people can actually read

**What this unlocks**
Your agent can write. After this skill, it knows how to lay things out: clear visual hierarchy, the right number of colors, bullets over paragraphs, signal over decoration. Whether you're building a dashboard, writing a document, or sharing a one-pager with your team, you get output that communicates instead of output that just fills space.

**Try it now**
- "Design a one-page status update for my team. Here's the info: [paste your notes]."
- "I'm building a simple dashboard. What's the right structure for showing three key metrics?"
- "Review this document I wrote. Does it lead with the action or bury it?"

**What to ask after**
- "Turn this into a visual summary I can share with my team."
- "This feels too dense. How do I cut it down without losing the key point?"
- "Build me a simple HTML dashboard showing this data."
- "I need to present this to an executive. What's the right format?"
- "Audit this document: is anything competing for attention that shouldn't be?"

**Tune it to your world**
Tell your agent your audience and your platform. A dashboard for a weekly team meeting is different from a one-pager for an executive. Specify once ("my team uses Teams, not slides") and the agent adjusts its defaults every time.
