# Teach your agent to report what the data shows, not what sounds right

**What this unlocks**
Your agent stops giving you conclusions disguised as data. After this skill, every metric comes with a denominator ("7 of 2,921" not "7"), every finding comes with a stated assumption, and every score comes with a plain-language explanation of what it means. You get numbers you can make decisions with, not numbers you have to second-guess.

**Try it now**
- "I have these numbers: [paste]. What does the data actually show versus what I might be inferring from it?"
- "Help me build a simple tracking table for this project. What metric should I use?"
- "This result surprised me: [describe]. What should I check before trusting it?"

**What to ask after**
- "What assumption is baked into this number that could make it misleading?"
- "Set up a rubric for scoring this before I run the evaluation."
- "Show me the distribution, not just the average."
- "What would I need to see to know this trend is real and not noise?"
- "I need to report this metric to my team. What context do they need to interpret it correctly?"

**Tune it to your world**
Tell your agent which metrics you track regularly and what "healthy" looks like for each one. Give it your team's scorecards or key dashboards and it will use your baseline, not a generic one.
